## general_clues

The coverage in Iraq is limited to a single trekker in the Iraqi National Museum in Baghdad.

All coverage is taken by a trekker with a camera similar to Gen 2 but with distinctly worse image quality and colour. This coverage tends to be overexposed, even becoming blindingly so at some points. NOTE: Sometimes referred to as Gen 1.5.

The trekker blur has a well-defined, pointed shape.

The only piece of coverage outdoors is a small patio with a diagonal walkway. In this patio you can see many date palms. This is the only piece of the coverage that can rarely appear on serious maps.

The inside of the museum features statues, steles, a variety of artefacts, and a few back rooms.
