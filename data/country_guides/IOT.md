## general_clues

The British Indian Ocean Territory is a United Kingdom overseas territory in the Indian Ocean. As such, it is counted as the UK for country streaks purposes.

None of the coverage is considered pinpointable, and as a result you are unlikely to get it in most community-made maps.

Coverage was taken with a Generation 3 trekker. Coverage is mostly found on small atolls in the northern half of the territory.

A large portion of coverage is on sand beaches with many palm trees.

Coastal palm forests also comprise a significant portion of the coverage. Almost exclusively coconut palms.

Mangroves can be found on several of the atolls.

Some larger islands have grassy fields surrounded by bushes and palms.

The trekker can occasionally be found walking offshore in the water.

## spotlight

Coin du Mire is a small island with a dark rocky shore and a grassy field with no bushes or palm trees.

A white medium-sized shipwreck can be found on the beach at the eastern tip of Nelsons Island.
