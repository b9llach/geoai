## general_clues

Isle of Man is a British Crown dependency, situated between the United Kingdom and Ireland. For country streaks purposes, it is counted as the United Kingdom.

Coverage on the Isle of Man is limited to Generation 2. This camera can be recognized by the lower camera quality and circular blur above and below. Colours will also seem slightly off.

Similar to the United Kingdom, the Isle of Man uses white front licence plates with yellow rears, but with a red strip on the left.

The general landscape consists of hilly farmland, and narrow roads passing through them.

Houses with whitewashed walls and entrance walls are a common sight, similar to Ireland.

Striped kerbs can sometimes be seen along main roads.

Blue bollards can commonly be found in urban areas.

Gorse plants (Ulex), recognized by their bright yellow flowers, are very common on the Isle of Man. NOTE: Gorse plants can also be found in other rugged areas of the UK and Ireland, such as the Scottish Highlands.

## regional_clues

Isle of Man is generally quite hilly, with a few exceptions.

Northern Isle of Man is very flat in comparison to the rest of the island. Usually, mountains are visible to the south.

A 'twig smudge' can be seen on the left side of the camera lens in southern Isle of Man.

Douglas, the capital and largest town, is recognized by rows of terraced houses with higher population density.

Laxey is a town located in a northwest-southeast valley.

Generic lamp design with a blue base and a small blue sticker can be found on most of the island, except around Douglas, and some parts northeast and south. The top 2 letters can narrow your location to 1 of 21 parishes.

All green lamps are commonly found in Ramsey and Castletown, more rarely in Malew, Santon, and Garff parishes.

A small white sticker with 2 green numbers is on lamps in Douglas.

A small yellow sticker is on lamps in southern Onchan. The 2 letters are initials of the road name.

Many street signs in Ramsey feature the oval shaped coat of arms.

The coat of arms is placed above the text on many signs in Port Erin.

## spotlight

The Ronaldsway Airport can be recognized by the large, flat and open field with a boundary fence.

The Peel castle, sitting atop an island, can unsurprisingly be seen in Peel.
