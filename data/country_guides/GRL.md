## general_clues

Coverage in Greenland is divided into well over fifty small disjoint parts, with different weather conditions, seasons, landmarks and so on. Although the largest concentration is found on the southern half of the west coast, coverage can be found scattered along a lot of the coast.

All coverage in Greenland was taken with a Generation 3 trekker camera, which was often attached to a variety of vehicles.

Most locations with coverage will have their own odd and unique Street View vehicles, ranging from a red tractor to a snowmobile with a large box at the front.

Greenlandic architecture mainly consists of small, colourful, simple looking wooden houses, which even in cities are typically placed quite sparsely. The major cities have some larger buildings, which normally look fairly minimalistic.

The Greenlandic landscape is very barren, with no trees or taller vegetation and often featuring snow. Mountains and hills are common, with very limited flat areas. Almost all coverage was taken in coastal areas, so fjords and visible coastlines are very common.

Boat trekkers are very common in Greenland, featuring a variety of different boats.

## regional_clues

Major land coverage:

Nuuk: largest city by far. Recognized by the grey Street View car.

Sisimiut: black snowmobile with side mirrors pointing straight up. Coverage is generally very overcast and grey.

Ilulissat: green ATV with black bars and the driver wearing a beige hat. Coverage is fairly sunny and bright.

Qaqortoq: red rectangular car. Coverage is extremely sunny and the area is greener and less barren than most of the country.

Aasiaat: windshield with shiny red, white and black reflection. Large, round side mirrors. Very sunny with thick, thawing snow along most of the roads.

Tasiilaq: ATV with large grey handguards on either side. Coverage has a lot of very thin clouds and the area is somewhat green.

Uummannaq: sharp mountain to the north and a green snowmobile with large black side mirrors.

Narsaq: thin antenna seen on the right side of the car. Very sunny and fairly green.

Minor land coverage:

Maniitsoq: tractor. Overcast and fairly snowy.

Narsarsuaq: driver wearing a black hoodie. Close to sunset on a very sunny day. A fair amount of small bushes along the road, somewhat unusual in Greenland.

Across the fjord from Narsarsuaq: black ATV with orange and purple bag in the front. Tall green grass and unusually red dirt for Greenland.

Qeqertarsuaq: blue ATV with wide side mirrors. Fairly overcast with yellow grass along the roads.

Ittoqqortoormiit: brown wooden box at the front of the ATV and the driver wearing a purple beanie. Ground mostly grey and stony, very sunny.

Igaliku: wide, snow-covered mountain range to the northeast which looks very bright due to the setting sun. Green ATV.

Paamiut: round, grey car which is often only barely visible. Thin clouds, quite bright. Foliage is yellow and green grass with reddish moss.

Kangerlussuaq: many streaky clouds in the sky combined with significant dryness. Many small bushes, sandy soil with almost arid climate.

The road east of Kangerlussuaq is easily recognized by the large white truck.

The coverage connecting Sisimiut and Kangerlussuaq has thick layer of snow on and around the road.

Saattut: wide mountain range shrouded in clouds, on the other side of a fjord. Dark, muddy dirt roads. Foliage is mainly yellow grass tufts.

Kulusuk island: round, red ATV. Pyramid shaped mountain often seen to the northeast. Right after sunset on a clear day.

Kangaatsiaq: large amount of thawing snow on the side of the roads. Sun setting, large body of water to the west.

Ikerasak: large monolithic mountain to the west.

Ukkusissat: located below a tall cliffside to the east with mountains towering above.

Kuummiit: two distinct mountains, sharp one to the east and rounder one to the north. Slightly dark due to clouds and sun setting.

Tiilerilaaq: houses are commonly grey rather than colourful. Ground is almost entirely smooth, light grey rocks.

Niaqornat: large, dark grey rocks towering over the village.

Siorapaluk: sandy, pyramid-shaped mountains to the north combined with a wide sand beach below the village.

Southwest of Qaanaaq: icebergs in a large body of water, wide mountain range with bright white snow on the other side.

Kapisillit (NE of Nuuk): red and black ATV. Many mountains in the distance covered in a layer of snow.

Kangerluk: evenly tall mountains with a particular shape on the other side of the fjord.

East of Nuuk: large number of sharp mountains, powdered in snow. Somewhat dark due to thick clouds and the sun being hidden.

About 20 km northeast of Narsarsuaq: small piece of coverage with large ice blocks in every direction.

Upernavik: airport runway is covered on Street View, by far the widest paved road found on Street View in Greenland.
