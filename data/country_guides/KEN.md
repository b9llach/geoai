## general_clues

Kenya has many unique Google cars, almost all of which will have visible car meta. In Generation 3 you will typically see a car with a roof rack and a snorkel. In Generation 4 you will always have a large pickup truck, either white or grey. The grey car comes in two slightly different variations. It is the only country other than Mongolia with a snorkel. NOTE: National Park Cars in other countries can have a snorkel.

On a lot of the coverage you will see a grey Toyota SUV following the Street View car.

Kenya uses long white front licence plates. The rear counterpart is square and yellow.

The mobile network brand Safaricom is unique to Kenya, and almost every city has one location.

Outer lines in Kenya will always be solid white, while inner lines will be yellow and either solid or dashed.

Directional signs are green with a white border and text. They often display road numbers in white or yellow.

While infrequent, road signs in Kenya will mostly be red and white.

Upon closer inspection of signs, billboards and posters, you will notice the county name is often written. Therefore knowledge of Kenyan counties can be extremely useful.

Repurposed vans can be found all over Kenya. NOTE: Similar vans can also be found in Uganda but the design of the vehicle wrap is clearly different.

Many species of eucalyptus are commonly found in Kenya. Identified by long, often clumped leaves and smooth bark.

While the vast majority of Kenyan car coverage has a visible Street View car, it is possible to find Generation 3 coverage with no visible car.

## regional_clues

Most of the coverage in Kenya outside of the southwest is limited to main roads.

The natural formation of land has great diversity between regions. The east and large portions of the north are extremely flat. The further southwest you travel, the higher the elevation becomes on average.

Kenya has distinct regional climates: Humid, Semi-Arid, Arid, Very-Arid. Many transitional climates exist.

This Generation 3 grey car with a large snorkel is mostly found in the west, but can also be found in Mombasa and Nairobi areas, and regions east of Nairobi near Kitui.

A Generation 3 black car with a small snorkel is mostly found in the east of the country. Also north and south of Nairobi.

Kenya has mostly Generation 4 coverage covered by a large grey Google car, with or without a snorkel.

Pieces of white tape on the roof of the Google car can be seen in a lot of coverage north and south of Nairobi.

The A109 between Mombasa and Nairobi has light orange dirt and many hills around the road and in the distance. You will see many transportation trucks delivering various goods. Most of the coverage between Mombasa and Emali also has a unique car with a cord on the left.

Mombasa and the other coastal towns are easily identified by white sandy soil, palm trees and coastal feel.

The far west areas (northwest and southwest of Kisumu) are the most agricultural parts. Often farms, grassy hills, and tall eucalyptus trees.

The area around Kitui is semi-arid, usually mountainous, with light orange, sandy dirt.

The northwest part of the country has very limited coverage, most on the A1 or in Sibiloi National Park. Faintly tan soil and sparse low trees. A lot of coverage is very flat.

White flowers, especially in large numbers, are commonly found east of Chyulu Hills National Park, in Tsavo National Park, and near A109 south of Nairobi.

Kenya has many unique Google cars in and around its National Parks and Reserves. Most have a snorkel.

## spotlight

The B9 in the Northeast is entirely unpaved with bright reddish orange dirt and many trees surrounding the road. Most very flat, but western end can have hills and mountains with more sandy soil.

The A2 between Isiolo and Marsabit has many plants alongside the road and large mountains often visible in the distance.

North of Marsabit is much flatter, with some mountains in the distance.

The A3 north of Garissa is very unique, with light coloured soil and many green trees on either side.

The western part of the C34 is a flat, east-to-west road. Two long, straight sections with abundance of sugar cane. Large mountain range visible to the north.

A car covered in specks of dirt is unique to stretches of A3, B9, and the Habaswein-Dadaab Road connecting them.

Nairobi is the largest city and capital. The only city with a considerable amount of skyscrapers. Highly developed but densely populated and surrounded by small, poor settlements.

In a lot of coverage north of Mt Kilimanjaro, the mountain is visible to the south, usually obscured by clouds. In Oloitoktok, the mountain is often visible directly to the south.

Homa Bay has a protruding hill to the east that is often visible.

Mara Triangle has no considerable mountains and most of it is flat or hilly. White car found in other Kenyan National Parks. Generation 4 coverage exists.

Amboseli National Park: grey car with small snorkel. Very flat with tan grass, few trees, dried up lake beds. Always see base of Kilimanjaro to the south, mostly obscured by clouds.

Aberdare National Park: grey and brown car with large snorkel. Almost all coverage outside the National Park, only two roads within the park covered. The main road through the park has rubber bands on the roof rack.

Between Kitale and Mt Elgon: same Aberdare car but always with rubber bands. Differentiated from Aberdare by redder dirt and greener landscape.

Around Kerio and Lodwar: same car as Aberdare National Park, but very sandy, dry, little vegetation. May also be in Generation 3 without car meta.

The B8 just north of Kibaoni has many succulents on the side of the road.
